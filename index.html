The calculator you are about to use will provide a roughly estimated outline of a client’s financial needs over time based the information and reasonable assumptions related to the client’s expenses, income and assets. Please note that this calculator is only an estimate of future financial needs extrapolated out over the course of time. The results it provides are based on your inputs and your assumptions about your client’s financial needs and resources now and in the future. Although these may be appropriate assumptions based on circumstances as they currently exist, neither you nor the calculator can predict future critical events such as remarriage, divorce, unexpected job loss, demotions, promotions, major market corrections, major medical events, to name a few. Therefore, this calculator should be used for demonstration and educational purposes only. The client must understand that the results this calculator provides should in no way be considered legal, tax or financial planning advice. The results are not a substitute for professional financial planning or tax advice.

This calculator is best used to argue a client’s case for alimony, spousal maintenance, child support, or a disproportionate share of marital or community property. It is effective and appropriate for that purpose. Although it is possible in the absence of professional financial advice to use it to help a client understand the approximated potential long-term effects of certain current or future financial choices, this should be done carefully. Careful consideration should be given as to whether and how to present certain results to clients, as a pessimistic or bleak financial outlook may unnecessarily alarm some clients, making even legally advantageous settlements more difficult for the client to accept.
