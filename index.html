Please note that this calculator is only an estimate of future financial needs extrapolated out over the course of time. The client must understand that the results this calculator provides should in no way be considered legal, tax or financial planning advice. The results are not a substitute for professional financial planning or tax advice.

This calculator is best used to argue a client’s case for alimony, spousal maintenance, child support, or a disproportionate share of marital or community property. It is effective and appropriate for that purpose. Although it is possible in the absence of professional financial advice to use it to help a client understand the approximated potential long-term effects of certain current or future financial choices, this should be done carefully. Careful consideration should be given as to whether and how to present certain results to clients, as a pessimistic or bleak financial outlook may unnecessarily alarm some clients, making even legally advantageous settlements more difficult for the client to accept.

INCOME FROM EMPLOYMENT

Employment income increases over time due to a host of different factors, which are of course different fo each individual case. These factors may include inflationary, cost of living, or performance raises, job promotions, company success, etc. This is of course speculative, and for most situations, it is likely best to estimate increases in income conservatively. However to provide some context, according to the Congressional Budget Office, the real median averagefamily income in the United States increased by an average of approximately 1.45% per year from 1979-2011, although the percentage changed widely when accounting for different lifestyle factors such as level of education, age, race, etc.
Lump sum increases could be due the client anticipating the completion of an educational track, a major job promotion, etc. As always, it is best to be conservative when entering or estimated the time such event(s) would take place, as well as any corresponding increase in income. If this is a temporary change, such as a temporary decrease due to other obligations, please indicate that there will be two changes – both the decrease, and the return to the normal salary.
